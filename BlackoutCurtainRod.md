# Hang Blackout Curtain Rod

To hang blackout curtains effectively, you must prioritize **light sealing**
over standard decorative placement. Because these curtains are heavier and
designed to block 100% of light, the rod placement and hardware type are
critical to preventing gaps.

## Optimal Rod Placement for Total Darkness

The most common mistake is mounting the rod too close to the window frame, which
allows light to leak from the top and sides. For blackout curtains, you must
extend the coverage significantly:

* **Height:** Mount the brackets **4 to 6 inches above** the window frame. For a
  more dramatic look and to eliminate top gaps, you can mount them even higher,
  just 1–2 inches below the ceiling.
* **Width:** Extend the rod **6 to 8 inches beyond** each side of the window
  frame (wider than the standard 3–4 inches). This ensures that when the
  curtains are closed, the fabric overlaps the wall rather than stopping at the
  glass edge, sealing out side light.
* **Overlap:** Ensure your curtain panels are wide enough (1.5 to 2 times the
  window width) so they overlap each other in the center by at least 2–3 inches.

## Essential Hardware: Wrap-Around Rods

Standard straight rods often leave a gap between the curtain edge and the wall.
For blackout purposes, **wrap-around (return) rods** are highly recommended.
These rods curve at the ends, pressing the curtain fabric flush against the wall
to eliminate side light leaks entirely.

* **Sturdiness:** Blackout curtains are significantly heavier than standard
  sheers. Use a sturdy metal rod and ensure brackets are screwed into wall studs
  or use heavy-duty drywall anchors to prevent sagging.
* **Center Support:** If your window is wider than 60 inches (5 feet), install a
  **center support bracket** to prevent the rod from bowing under the weight of
  the fabric.

## Step-by-Step Installation

1. **Measure and Mark:** Determine your height (4–6" above frame) and width (
   6–8" past frame on each side). Use a level to ensure your bracket marks are
   perfectly even.
2. **Drill and Anchor:** Pre-drill holes at your marks. If not hitting a stud,
   insert appropriate wall anchors.
3. **Mount Brackets:** Secure the brackets firmly. Double-check they are level.
4. **Hang Curtains First:** Slide the curtains onto the rod *before* mounting it
   to the brackets. This is much easier than trying to thread heavy fabric onto
   a mounted rod.
5. **Install Rod:** Place the rod onto the brackets. If using a wrap-around rod,
   ensure the curve directs the fabric toward the wall.
6. **Seal Gaps:** If light still leaks from the sides, consider adding **Velcro
   strips** or **magnetic tape** to the curtain edges and attaching them to the
   wall. For the top, a cornice board or valance can block remaining light.
