# Zeer Pot

A [zeer pot](https://thehomesteadinghippy.com/diy-zeer-pot/) (also known as a
**pot-in-pot refrigerator** or **clay pot cooler**) is a non-electric
refrigeration device that uses **evaporative cooling** to preserve food. It is
particularly effective in hot, dry climates where electricity is scarce or
unreliable.

## How It Works

The zeer pot operates on the principle of **evaporative cooling**. When water
evaporates, it absorbs heat from its surroundings (latent heat of vaporization),
lowering the temperature of the remaining system.

The device consists of:

* **Two porous clay pots**: A smaller inner pot placed inside a larger outer
  pot.
* **Wet sand**: The space between the two pots is filled with moist sand, which
  acts as a water reservoir and wick.
* **Wet cloth or lid**: Covers the top to protect contents and maintain
  humidity.

As water from the sand seeps through the porous outer pot and evaporates into
the surrounding air, it draws heat away from the inner pot. This process can
lower the internal temperature by **10 to 15°C (18 to 27°F)** below the ambient
temperature, and in ideal conditions (dry air with a breeze), up to **40°F
(approx. 22°C)** cooler.

## Construction and Maintenance

Building a zeer pot requires minimal resources.

1. Select two unglazed terracotta pots of different sizes.
2. Place the smaller pot inside the larger one.
3. Fill the gap with sand and saturate it with water.
4. Cover the top with a damp cloth or lid.

**Maintenance** involves adding water to the sand **once or twice daily** to
ensure continuous evaporation. The device must be placed in a **shady,
well-ventilated area** to maximize airflow and evaporation efficiency.

## Ideal Conditions and Limitations

The effectiveness of a zeer pot depends heavily on environmental factors.  

* **Low Humidity**: Works best when relative humidity is below 40%. High
  humidity prevents evaporation, rendering the device ineffective.
* **High Temperature**: Requires ambient temperatures above 77°F (25°C) to
  function optimally.
* **Airflow**: A breeze or fan significantly enhances cooling by accelerating
  evaporation.

It is suitable for storing vegetables (e.g., tomatoes, eggplants, leafy greens)
and fruits, extending their shelf life from a few days to **up to 20 days**.
However, it cannot reach temperatures low enough for meat, dairy, or medicines
requiring sustained cooling below 20°C.

## Historical and Social Impact

The technology dates back to **2500 BC in Egypt** and **3000 BC in the Indus
Valley Civilization**. In modern times, it was popularized in the 1990s by
Nigerian teacher **Mohammed Bah Abba**, who mass-produced the devices to help
rural farmers reduce food spoilage. His work earned him the **Rolex Award for
Enterprise** in 2001.

The zeer pot provides significant social benefits:

* **Economic**: Farmers can sell produce at higher prices by avoiding spoilage
  rushes.
* **Nutritional**: Families enjoy a more varied diet year-round.
* **Employment**: Local pot makers gain jobs producing the coolers.
